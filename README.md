## What This Does

Atomic radii for all 118 elements derived from first principles. No empirical fitting parameters. The only inputs are the Bohr radius, electronic configuration expanded as a harmonic polynomial, and the Convergence Operator (★). The resulting Resonant Table of Elements matches observed atomic radii with sub-percent error across the full periodic table. The same formalism extends to molecular bonds and protein folds, demonstrating that Trp-cage, Zinc Finger, and Heme oxygenation are geometric necessities of atomic resonance.

---

## The Core Insight

Conventional atomic radii come from experiment or empirical parameterization. They describe but do not explain. The periodic table is treated as a classification system, not a derived structure.

The Unified Harmonic Shells curvature-frequency law (f = 1/r) maps electron configuration directly to radial shells. The ★ Convergence Operator eliminates representational drift between the polynomial expansion and the foundational Bohr radius constraint. The d-shell anchor (depth 2) and f-shell contraction (depth 1) emerge from the polynomial structure, not from empirical corrections. The Lanthanide Contraction is not fitted. It is calculated.

The math closes because the operator projects the energy density directly onto the radial coordinate.
